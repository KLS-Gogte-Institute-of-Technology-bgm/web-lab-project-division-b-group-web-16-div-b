About Our Webpage

Till 19th century tourism in Himachal Pradesh was restricted only to a limited government of pilgrims to a few spiritual destinations in around the health only when the British established their chain of hill stations did tourism receive recognization in in the state tourism activity received a shot in the alarm 1 British declared Shimla the summer capital of India in 1864 post-independence more investment in the infrastructure sector led to the opening of tourism of the state however the biggest boost tourism occur in the middle of 80s and 90s

While traditionally Himachal was known as the summer destinations the department of tourism and Civil Aviation has taken special efforts to break the seasonality factor and has developed a diversified tourism products to attract tourists in other seasons to now Himachal is known as a destination for all seasons for all reasons in fact the department has led a special emphasis on the development of activity based tourism and opening up of new destinations to promote tourism in the countryside and to unexplored areas appropriate infrastructure is being developed within available resources by focusing on quality tourist the department aimed at promoting Sustainable Tourism and encouraging private sector to develop tourism related infrastructure in the state without disturbing the existing Ecology and environment.
